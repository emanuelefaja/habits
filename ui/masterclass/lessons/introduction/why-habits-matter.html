Why Habits Matter

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Small changes in daily routines don't seem significant at first, but over time, they can have a remarkable impact on your life.

The Power of Small Changes

If you improve just 1% each day for a year, you'll end up 37 times better by the end. Conversely, if you get 1% worse, you'll decline nearly all the way to zero.

Key Insight

Success is the product of daily habits—not once-in-a-lifetime transformations. You get what you repeat.

Why Habits Define Us

Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you wish to become. Your identity emerges out of your habits. Every belief, including beliefs about yourself, is learned and conditioned through experience.

  • Your habits shape your identity
  • Your identity shapes your habits
  • This two-way street is the foundation of lasting change
"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit." — Aristotle

Exercise: Habit Audit

Take a moment to identify three habits that have positively shaped your life so far. Then, consider three habits you'd like to develop in the future.

Reflection questions:

  1. What does your current set of habits say about your identity?
  2. What kind of person do you want to become?
  3. What habits would that type of person have?
